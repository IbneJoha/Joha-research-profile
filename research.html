Research

Md. Ibne Joha's research stands at the intersection of AI, edge computing, and energy systems, addressing the critical challenges of real-time performance, scalability, and security in Industrial IoT environments. His innovative integration of deep learning techniques with NILM, forecasting, anomaly detection, object tracking, and edge computing underscores his commitment to building intelligent, resource-efficient energy infrastructures. By optimizing model architectures for deployment on edge devices, he effectively bridges the gap between theoretical advancements and real-world applicability. His work not only enhances system reliability and energy forecasting or disaggregation accuracy but also sets a benchmark for secure and sustainable AIoT or edge deployments. Joha continues to pursue impactful, interdisciplinary research that advances the frontiers of smart energy management, energy efficiency, object tracking, and edge or embedded AI solutions.

One-Day Ahead Solar PV Power Generation Forecasting (Government and Company Projects)

Advanced AI models, including deep learning architectures, have been deployed across 10 PV sites to accurately forecast solar power generation. The models use key features such as temperature, wind speed, humidity, cloud cover, snowfall, and power aggregation, among others, to analyze historical data, ensuring precise energy predictions. These models achieve an RMSE of less than 8%, demonstrating high forecasting accuracy.

AI-Driven Real-Time Load Forecasting and Anomaly Detection in a Secure IIoT Environment

This study presents a secure IIoT framework for real-time energy optimization, using a TCN-GRU-Attention model to predict active and reactive loads with high accuracy. An optimized Isolation Forest model detects anomalies, achieving 95% Precision, 98% Recall, 96% F1 Score, and near 100% Accuracy. The system is optimized for deployment on edge computing devices (e.g., SBC) and cloud servers. The system is secured with TLS, SSL, and encrypted credentials for reliable industrial operations.

An Efficient AI Model for Real-time Non-Intrusive Load Monitoring within a Secure Edge Computing Environment

The proposed ARBGTU-Net-NILM model enhances NILM performance through accurate and robust multi-appliance disaggregation in noisy, real-world conditions. It achieves a 51.27% F1-score improvement and reduces MAE by 38.28% and SAE by 89.83% compared to the state-of-the-art. Designed for deployment on edge devices, it ensures secure operation with TLS/SSL and encrypted credentials and performs effectively on both REDD and real-time datasets.

Comparative Analysis of AI Models for Real-Time IoT-based Environmental Monitoring and Power Forecasting

This study presents an IoT platform for real-time environmental and energy data collection, AI model for forecasting, and secure data transmission. A hybrid LSTM-GRU model is proposed to improve environmental monitoring and power predictions by combining LSTM‚Äôs strength in sequential data with GRU‚Äôs ability to capture long-term dependencies efficiently. Explainable AI for Non-intrusive Load Monitoring and Anomaly Detection

This study introduces XAI to translate load disaggregation outputs into clear appliance-level monitoring insights by comparing predicted power values with predefined thresholds to classify on/off states. It also improves anomaly detection by identifying unusual consumption patterns, such as unexpected spikes or unusual behavior, and generating concise, human-readable explanations. By integrating explainable AI, the system provides reliable predictions, actionable energy recommendations, and builds user trust in automated monitoring.

Federated Learning concept for Load Forecasting, Disaggregation, Anomaly, or Intrusion Detection. (Ongoing)

This study explores the application of Federated Learning (FL) for decentralized training of AI models across distributed edge nodes, with a focus on preserving data privacy and minimizing communication overhead. In scenarios such as load forecasting, load disaggregation, anomaly detection, and intrusion detection, FL is utilized to facilitate collaborative model development without requiring raw data transfer to centralized servers, thereby ensuring data security and regulatory compliance. By reducing reliance on central infrastructure and enabling secure model aggregation, system resilience is enhanced, optimal scheduling is supported, and efficient resource management is achieved.

A RNN-CNN Based Parallel Hybrid Approach for Battery SoC Estimation Under Varying Temperatures and Noisy Conditions

This study proposes a novel hybrid RNN-CNN-based model for accurate state of charge (SoC) estimation in Li-ion batteries used in electric vehicles (EVs). It is trained on voltage, current, and temperature data and performs well across varying operating conditions. Performance metrics show an average RMSE of 0.512%, MAE of 0.354%, MaxE of 1.98%, and R¬≤ of 99.94%. The model outperforms an attention-based TCN (ATCN) model, offering a reliable method for SoC estimation.

Enhancing Optical Camera Communication Performance for Collaborative Communication Using Positioning Information (Military Drone OCC Project)

This study proposed a novel DCC-OOK modulation scheme to enable point-to-point and bidirectional communication in OCC by leveraging relative localization using multi-sensor fusion of LiDAR and camera data. The system accurately measured inter-device distances, applied color-coded modulation based on positioning, and achieved a data rate of 38.4 kbps with a BER of 0.03. This approach not only allows transmitters to selectively communicate with specific receivers but also paves the way for future multi-agent OCC systems with enhanced localization accuracy.

AI-Driven Integrated Gimbal and Zoom-Focus Control System for Enhanced OCC Performance (Military Drone OCC Project)

The proposed AI-driven system integrates real-time ROI extraction, deep learning-based object tracking, and fuzzy logic-based gimbal control to maintain stable OCC performance at target speeds up to 10 km/h. By combining deep learning with 5th-degree polynomial curve fitting, the system adaptively controls zoom and focus based on object motion, maintaining precise alignment and optimal image sharpness in real time. This unified approach enhances OCC performance by delivering intelligent, low-latency responses essential for fast-changing environments such as drone-based communication and mobile surveillance.

Enhancing Smart Grid Resilience Through Privacy-Preserving and Decentralized Technologies (Ongoing)

The integration of federated learning, blockchain, and AI-driven methodologies significantly strengthens cybersecurity, enabling robust intrusion detection and prevention while ensuring data privacy across smart grid infrastructures. This decentralized paradigm also facilitates secure energy trading, precise load forecasting, and optimal resource scheduling, thereby enhancing both operational efficiency and system resilience. Collectively, these advanced technologies support the development of reliable and scalable cyber-physical power systems, equipping the grid to effectively respond to evolving demands and security threats.
